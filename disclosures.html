How to read Sanket.

Last updated · June 2026

Please read this carefully. It explains what Sanket is, what it is not, and the risks involved in using it.

What Sanket is

Sanket is a software product that provides educational technical analysis and risk-management tools. It presents data, indicators, conviction scores, and illustrative position-sizing and exit calculations to help you think more clearly about a trade.

What Sanket is not

Sanket is not investment advice and not a recommendation to buy, sell, or hold any security. We are not registered with SEBI as an Investment Adviser or a Research Analyst. Nothing in the product should be taken as a personalised recommendation or a solicitation to transact in any security.

No guarantees, and real risk

Trading and investing in securities carries risk, including the risk of losing your entire capital. No analysis method removes that risk. Conviction scores are confidence levels, not predictions, and past performance never guarantees future results. Any figures shown in the product or on this site are illustrative and not a promise of any outcome.

Not personalised advice

Sanket does not assess your individual financial situation, goals, or risk tolerance, and does not provide advice tailored to you. Position-sizing figures are illustrative calculations based on inputs you provide, not a suitability assessment.

Your decisions, your broker

Sanket does not connect to, read, or trade from your broker account. You sign in to Sanket separately, and Sanket shows you analysis and order details only. Sanket does not place trades, access your holdings, move money, or act on your behalf. Every order is placed by you, in your own broker account, at your sole discretion.

Do your own research

Always do your own research and consult a SEBI-registered Investment Adviser or Research Analyst before making any investment decision. You are solely responsible for your trades and their outcomes.
