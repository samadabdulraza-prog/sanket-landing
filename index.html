Anatomy of a signal

Every call, decoded.

Nothing on a Sanket card is decoration. Here's what each line is telling you — and why it's there.

The call
Buy, wait, or pass. One word, no hedging. If the read isn't strong enough, the answer is "pass" — and that's a feature.
Conviction
How sure, honestly. A 0–100 score that's allowed to say "only moderate." A confident number you can't trust is worthless.
Three lenses
The why behind the score. Business, technical, and market — so you can see exactly what's driving the call, and what's holding it back.
Entry
Where the setup is valid. Not "buy at any price" — a specific level where the trade actually makes sense.
Stop
Your line in the sand. The price that says the idea was wrong. Decided before you enter, never moved in panic.
Size
Exactly how many shares. Sized so a stop-out costs a small, survivable slice of your capital — typically 1%.

Questions

The honest answers.

Is this investment advice?
No. Sanket is an educational technical-analysis and risk-management tool. It is not investment advice, not a recommendation to buy or sell any security, and we are not SEBI-registered. Every decision and every trade is yours. See the full Disclosures.
Does Sanket touch my broker or my money?
Never. Sanket doesn't connect to your broker, hold your funds, or place trades. It shows you analysis and order details only — you place every order yourself, in your own account, at your own discretion.
What happens when a call is wrong?
That's the whole reason the stop and the position size come first. Every call is sized so that being wrong costs a small, survivable slice of your capital — typically around 1%. A bad trade is meant to be a scratch, not a wipeout.
Do you guarantee returns?
No, and we never will. Trading carries real risk, including the loss of your capital. No method removes that. A conviction score is a confidence level, not a prediction, and past results never guarantee future ones.
What does it cost?
Nothing during the beta. We're focused on getting it right with our first 100 users. When we do introduce pricing, you'll know well in advance.
